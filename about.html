MetaVoxa

Research and advocacy platform · Founded October 2024 · Berlin, Germany

MetaVoxa is a research and advocacy platform at the intersection of healthcare, innovation, and strategy, founded by Dr. Martha Wuya Koroma in October 2024. Headquartered in Berlin, Germany, MetaVoxa operates through three interconnected layers:

MetaVoxa Press

Publishes essays on health, power, and people systems were never designed to see. Written by a physician who has been inside the system — and outside it.

MetaVoxa Atrium

A digital sanctuary and community space built as a world into the founder's brain, welcoming guests through rooms including the Salon (community chat), the Study Wing (research visualization), and the Glam Wing (artistic expression).

MetaVoxa Research

Investigates neurodivergence in African women, with current focus on West African born-and-raised neurodivergent women who migrated to Europe between ages 15–25. This work is conducted as part of a Dr. med. thesis and represents Phase 1 of the MetaVoxa global neurodivergence database.

Founder

Dr. Martha Wuya Koroma, MD candidate (graduating November 2026), BSc Business Administration, Postgraduate Cybersecurity. International Collaborator, The Lancet Psychiatry (2025). ICH-GCP certified. Clinical practice at Vivantes Hospital Berlin. Diplomatic experience across 8 countries.
